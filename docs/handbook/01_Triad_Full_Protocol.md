Executive Summary
=================
Triad Protocol is a privacy-first interdisciplinary community connecting Tech, Legal, and Media contributors. Members act under pseudonymous handles, earn XP for verified contribution, advance ranks, and may hold a persistent Founder meta-role (Leader/Co‑Leader). This file is the canonical reference for governance, operations, private media access, consent, and business rules.

1. Purpose, mission, & value
- Mission: Enable private, verifiable cross‑discipline collaboration that is fair, auditable and sustainable.
- Value: Members gain reputational currency, mentorship, and paid opportunities; clients get vetted teams; founders steward the platform.

2. Ranks & XP (high-level)
- Ranks: Rookie | Adept | Veteran | Commander | Overlord | Archon.
- XP threshold scale: XP_required = 100 × 10^(rank-1)
  - Rookie 0–99, Adept 100–999, Veteran 1,000–9,999, Commander 10,000–99,999, Overlord 100,000–999,999, Archon 1,000,000+.

3. Core principles
- Consent-first: members opt-in to projects and private channels.
- Separation of powers: XP/rank controls operational permission; Founder meta-role is stewardship and audit-limited.
- Auditability: awards, promotions, sanctions, emergency actions logged and reviewable.
- Abuse prevention: explicit SOPs, reporting channels, audits, caps/decay.

4. Founder (Dual-Title)
- Founder = persistent label (Leader / Co‑Leader) separate from rank.
- Privileges: ex-officio Governance seat, priority maintenance tickets, limited emergency actions. Emergency actions must be logged and reviewed within 72 hours.
- Limits: Founder does NOT override rank; cannot unmask identities unilaterally; succession/removal require high governance thresholds.

5. Private Media Channel (Rank ≥ Adept)
- Access: Rank ≥ Adept + media primer completion + explicit opt-in.
- Workflow: drafts in-channel → approvals → publish gates (routine vs high‑impact).
- Roles: Member, Campaign Lead (Rank ≥ Veteran or appointed), Moderator, Auditor.
- Legal/paid campaigns require legal review and explicit contributor opt-ins.

6. Beginner Launch (starter plan)
- Small cohort (5–12), short Bootcamp (1–3 hrs), mentor pairing, one micro-project (2–4 weeks), weekly short syncs, modest XP awards.
- Focus: learning, consistent evidence practice, refine checklists.

7. Business model highlights
- Revenue options: membership tiers, client marketplace fees, training/certification, grants.
- Payouts: platform fee (example 10–20%), escrow for milestones, transparent splits.
- Founder compensation recorded and approved by Governance.

8. Governance & SLAs
- Governance Board: Archons + rotating Overlord delegates + elected member representatives + external advisor(s).
- SLAs: promotion review ~14 days; incident triage initial response 48 hours; emergency review 72 hours.
- Audits: monthly reconciliations, random audits, periodic external audit.

9. Safety & legal posture
- TOS and Privacy Policy required before public launch.
- Identity unmasking only via quorum, counsel review, and documented justification.
- No illegal or harmful activity tolerated.
