% Triad Protocol — Ranks, Responsibilities & Promotion Paths
% Focused reference: what each rank *does*, what powers it holds, and how promotion works
% Date: 2026-05-01

Overview
========
This document lists the community ranks (Rookie → Archon), the practical responsibilities and powers associated with each rank, and the promotion requirements and process for moving between ranks. It is intentionally focused on rank roles and promotion mechanics only — no broader platform rules are included here.

Quick rank table (XP thresholds)
- Rookie: 0–99 XP
- Adept: 100–999 XP
- Veteran: 1,000–9,999 XP
- Commander: 10,000–99,999 XP
- Overlord: 100,000–999,999 XP
- Archon: 1,000,000+ XP

Note: Founder (Leader / Co‑Leader) is a persistent meta-role separate from rank. Founders retain their rank-based powers plus stewardship privileges; founder status does not replace promotion flows.

Rank details

1) Rookie (0–99 XP)
- Purpose: Learn the community, practice contribution process, and build evidence habits.
- Typical responsibilities
  - Complete Bootcamp / primer.
  - Do small tasks (documentation, minor fixes, short write-ups).
  - Participate in a mentor pairing and initial micro-project.
- Powers & privileges
  - Read and contribute to public threads and open issues.
  - Submit award requests and evidence.
  - Be assigned as contributor on small tasks or as an observer on projects.
- Typical activities that earn XP
  - Bootcamp completion, small task completions (10–25 XP), participation credits.
- Promotion (to Adept)
  - XP: reach ≥100 XP.
  - Evidence: Bootcamp completion + at least 2 verified small tasks (links and short summaries).
  - Time-in-rank: none required.
  - Reviewer: peer reviewer or assigned mentor sign-off.
  - Outcome: Adept privileges unlocked (including option to join private media channels after primer).

2) Adept (100–999 XP)
- Purpose: Independent contributor who reliably completes routine work and begins cross-discipline collaboration.
- Typical responsibilities
  - Complete moderate tasks; participate actively in team work.
  - Draft and iterate deliverables; respond to peer review.
  - Opt-in to Rank ≥ Adept private channels (media/project drafts) after media primer.
- Powers & privileges
  - Access to Rank ≥ Adept private channels (opt-in).
  - Ability to propose routine tasks and be nominated for small lead roles.
  - Vote/endorse in simple peer-verification flows (1 peer confirmation).
- Typical activities that earn XP
  - Moderate tasks, campaign assets, accepted task milestones, verified peer mentoring.
- Promotion (to Veteran)
  - XP: reach ≥1,000 XP.
  - Evidence: 3 medium tasks OR 1 project milestone + 1 medium task; 2 peer references recommended.
  - Time-in-rank: recommended min 3 months active as Adept.
  - Reviewer: discipline reviewer + panel (cross-discipline evidence when applicable).
  - Outcome: Veteran-level responsibilities (project ownership, higher-review authority).

3) Veteran (1,000–9,999 XP)
- Purpose: Project-level contributor and informal leader; takes responsibility for larger deliverables and mentoring others.
- Typical responsibilities
  - Lead significant sub-projects or modules.
  - Mentor Rookies/Adepts and verify peer work.
  - Act as senior reviewer for medium awards in their discipline.
- Powers & privileges
  - Serve as Campaign Lead on small-to-medium campaigns (with approvals).
  - Approve medium awards as a discipline peer reviewer.
  - Access to higher-permission project areas and cross-discipline collaborations.
- Typical activities that earn XP
  - Project milestones, leading features/modules, publishing reusable assets, verified mentorship outcomes.
- Promotion (to Commander)
  - XP: reach ≥10,000 XP.
  - Evidence: lead/co-lead at least one cross-discipline project with measurable outcomes; documented artifacts and stakeholder feedback.
  - Time-in-rank: recommended min 6 months active as Veteran.
  - Reviewer: multi-person panel including at least one Overlord/Commander-level reviewer and a discipline reviewer.
  - Outcome: Commander-level permissions and ability to form/approve persistent cross-discipline channels.

4) Commander (10,000–99,999 XP)
- Purpose: Senior operational leader who coordinates cross-discipline teams, approves larger projects, and enforces quality.
- Typical responsibilities
  - Propose and approve multi-week projects and campaigns.
  - Chair review panels for promotions and high-impact awards.
  - Manage project risk, timelines, and cross-team coordination.
- Powers & privileges
  - Create persistent cross-discipline channels (requires Commander-level sign-off).
  - Approve high-impact content (with required legal/media checks).
  - Serve on governance panels and nominate delegates.
  - Emergency operational privileges (e.g., temporary project freezes) within audit process.
- Typical activities that earn XP
  - Leading multiple projects, major client milestones, governance contributions, cross-discipline strategy.
- Promotion (to Overlord)
  - XP: reach ≥100,000 XP.
  - Evidence: multiple Commander-level projects showing organizational impact; governance/strategy contributions.
  - Time-in-rank: recommended min 12 months active as Commander.
  - Reviewer: Governance-level panel (Overlords, Archons, and elected reps).
  - Outcome: Overlord responsibilities and Governance participation.

5) Overlord (100,000–999,999 XP)
- Purpose: Executive steward — sustained leadership across projects, governance, and platform stewardship.
- Typical responsibilities
  - Shape long-term strategy and governance policy.
  - Lead or oversee large programs, client relationships, and platform stability.
  - Mentor and vet Commander-level leaders.
- Powers & privileges
  - Seat on Governance Board (ex-officio or elected roles depending on charter).
  - Approve major policy decisions, financial distributions, and high-risk engagements.
  - Lead external-facing statements and high-trust operations (with legal counsel).
- Typical activities that earn XP
  - Sustained multi-project leadership, governance workload, major client wins, platform stewardship.
- Promotion (to Archon)
  - XP: reach ≥1,000,000 XP.
  - Evidence: demonstrable system-level impact, governance leadership, external advisor endorsements.
  - Time-in-rank: multi-year track record expected; Board-level review.
  - Reviewer: Full Governance Board + external advisor input recommended.
  - Outcome: Archon-level authority and permanent Governance influence.

6) Archon (1,000,000+ XP)
- Purpose: Legacy leadership — shapes vision, long-term governance, and external strategy at scale.
- Typical responsibilities
  - Define long-term mission and governance direction.
  - Serve as final escalation for the most critical platform matters.
  - Represent the community externally where needed.
- Powers & privileges
  - Highest governance authority short of formal legal or corporate actions.
  - Instigate major structural changes, advise on identity-recovery precedents, and sign major platform statements.
- Typical activities that earn XP
  - Decade-scale or system-level contributions, creation of major protocols, repeat, proven governance leadership.
- Promotion / appointment
  - Rare, carefully reviewed promotion by full Governance Board and external advisors.
  - Requires overwhelming evidence of long-term leadership and community impact.

Promotion process (common steps)
1. Eligibility / Nomination
   - System auto-flags on XP threshold OR a peer/self nomination submits evidence package.
2. Evidence package
   - Deliverable links, 100-word summaries, reviewer sign-offs, client acceptance notes, peer references.
3. Panel review
   - Panel composition scales with rank: include discipline reviewers and at least one reviewer of equal/higher rank.
4. Decision & recording
   - Panel records decision, rationale, and required follow-ups; if approved, rank updated and audit log written.
5. Appeals
   - Candidate may appeal within the established appeals window; appeal heard by an independent panel.

Promotion checklist (concise)
- XP threshold met? (Yes/No)
- Evidence package complete? (deliverables, links, acceptance notes)
- Time-in-rank satisfied (where applicable)?
- Conflict-of-interest / reviewer availability checked?
- Panel convened and decision recorded?

Examples of evidence by rank
- Adept → Veteran: project milestone link + mentor sign-off + peer reference.
- Veteran → Commander: project repo + client acceptance email + cross-discipline artifacts + impact metrics.
- Commander → Overlord: multiple project dossiers + governance contributions + stakeholder references.
- Overlord → Archon: long-term governance records + external advisor letters + demonstrable platform changes.

Privileges / Role mapping (quick reference)
- Private media channel access: Adept+ (opt-in).
- Campaign Lead: Veteran+ (training recommended).
- Approve medium awards: Veteran (peer reviewer).
- Approve large awards / chair panels: Commander+.
- Governance seat: Overlord+ (or designated by charter).
- Founder meta-role: separate — founders keep their rank powers plus stewardship duties, but do not bypass promotion rules.

Notes & best practices
- Evidence quality > quantity: reviewers prioritize verifiable links, acceptance notes, and demonstrable outcomes.
- Time-in-rank requirements stabilize promotions and are enforcement discretion — panels may require demonstrated sustained activity.
- Promotions should be documented and auditable; all panel members and their voting should be recorded (handles and roles).
- Mentorship is expected: Veterans and above should support the development of Rookies/Adepts.
