# Triad Protocol — Quick Welcome (Plain Language)

Welcome to Triad Protocol. This one-page guide explains, in simple words, what the community is, how it works, what you need to do, and the basic rules.

---

## What is Triad Protocol?
Triad Protocol is a private community where people with skills in Tech, Legal, and Media work together using pseudonyms (handles). Members earn XP for verified work, move up through ranks, and can join private project or media groups — all while keeping their real identities protected unless strict rules apply.

---

## Why it exists
- Let people learn, collaborate, and earn without exposing personal identity.  
- Provide mentorship, verified reputation, and safe client work opportunities.  
- Keep governance and safety rules clear so the space stays fair and respectful.

---

## How it works (very short)
1. Join with a handle (nickname).  
2. Accept the Community Rules & Agreement.  
3. Complete a short Bootcamp (privacy + discipline primer).  
4. Do tasks or join projects and upload evidence.  
5. Earn XP when work is verified → move up ranks (Rookie → Adept → Veteran → ...).  
6. At Rank 2 (Adept) you can opt into private media/project channels.

---

## Simple Checklist to Start
- [ ] Pick a handle (public name).  
- [ ] Read & accept the Community Rules (checkbox form).  
- [ ] Complete the Bootcamp (1–3 hours).  
- [ ] Get paired with a mentor for the first 30 days.  
- [ ] Join a micro-project or do a small task and submit evidence.

---

## XP & Ranks — Plain Explanation
- XP = points for contribution. Small tasks = small XP, bigger milestones = more XP.  
- Example: finish Bootcamp → some XP; finish a project milestone → much more XP.  
- 100 XP → Adept (unlocks private groups). Keep contributing to climb higher.  
- The system has caps and checks to prevent gaming.

---

## Private Media / Project Channel (Rank ≥ Adept)
- Must opt in and finish the media primer.  
- Post drafts in the channel first; a Campaign Lead or reviewer must approve public posts.  
- No doxxing, impersonation, or deceptive campaigns.  
- Upload analytics/evidence to earn XP from campaigns.

---

## Safety & Reporting (Simple)
- If someone harasses or breaks rules, use the anonymous report form.  
- Reports are triaged quickly (initial response target: 48 hours).  
- The platform can temporarily freeze accounts or posting while investigating.  
- Appeals are available if you disagree with a decision.

---

## What "Founder / Leader" Means
- Founder (Leader / Co‑Leader) is a label for people who helped create or maintain the platform.  
- Founders can take emergency actions, but those actions are logged and reviewed by Governance within a short time (e.g., 72 hours).  
- Founders cannot unmask members or punish people alone.

---

## Beginner Plan (4–6 weeks)
- Week 1: Onboarding, Bootcamp, mentor pairing.  
- Weeks 2–3: Micro-project with 2–4 people (3–5 hours/week).  
- Week 4: Present deliverable, receive small XP awards, share feedback.

---

## What You Get Out of It
- Practice working in interdisciplinary teams.  
- A verified, pseudonymous portfolio of your contributions.  
- Mentorship and the chance to earn on paid client projects later.  
- A governed, safer space for collaboration.

---

## Not Allowed (Short List)
- No doxxing (revealing private identities).  
- No coercion or secret control.  
- No illegal activity, impersonation, or deceptive campaigns.  
- No gaming the XP system.

---

## Quick FAQ
- Q: How do I earn my first XP?  
  A: Complete the Bootcamp and submit a small task with evidence for peer review.

- Q: Can I leave a project?  
  A: Yes — you can withdraw from non-critical tasks with the notice rules in the project agreement.

- Q: Will my real identity ever be revealed?  
  A: Only in exceptional cases (lawful request, imminent harm, or verified large fraud), after a formal governance and legal process.

- Q: I’m not technical — can I join?  
  A: Yes. Media and Legal tracks have non-technical tasks. Mentors help you find suitable work.
